SELF-REGULATED LEARNING (SRL) AND ACADEMIC PROCRASTINATION AMONG COLLEGE STUDENT

Rizka P. R. Annisab
Student, Department of Psychology, Universitas Islam Indonesia, Indonesia
Email: rizkaprameta@gmail.com
Muhammad I. A. Majid
Student, Department of Psychology, Universitas Islam Indonesia, Indonesia
Nashif A. R. Fanani
Student, Department of Psychology, Universitas Islam Indonesia, Indonesia
Nur Widiasmara
Lecture, Department of Psychology, Universitas Islam Indonesia, Indonesia

Abstract: Students often have difficulties working on their college assignments on time. The number of assignments and activities outside the lecture make students have to divided their time well. However, many students also chose to postponed their duties until the deadline runs out, as known as procrastination. On the other hand, students who have good self-regulation can manage their tasks optimally and on time. This study was aimed to examined the relationship between self-regulated learning with academic procrastination. Respondents in this study were psychology students of Universitas Islam Indonesia class of 2015, 2016 and 2017, with 64 students consisting of 32 women and 32 men. Data retrieval method in this study used a scale Self-Regulation of Learning Self Report Scale (SRL-SRS) and Shortened Version of The Procrastination Scale. The results of the analysis showing correlation of t = -.276. This shows that there is a significant negative relationship between self-regulated learning with academic procrastination in psychology students of Universitas Islam Indonesia. This means that the higher student’s self-regulated learning, the lower is the academic procrastination.

Keywords: Academic procrastination, psychology student, self-regulated learning (SRL).

Abstract: The purpose of this paper is to investigate the aspects of basic word orders and their variations in English and Korean, and the interactions with the iconicity of distance principle for the basic word orders and their variations. SACRIFICIAL MOTHERHOOD OR EMPOWERMENT: WHAT DO NARRATIONS FROM SANI YI WOMEN EMBROIDERY TRADERS TELL US?

Guo Sisi
Ph.D. Candidate, Department of Women’s and Gender Studies, Faculty of Social Sciences, Chiang Mai University, Thailand

Abstract: This paper attempts to explore the process in which Sani Yi women embroidery traders in Shilin, Yunnan Province, China, empower themselves through economic activities engagement. In the past, Sani Yi men were viewed as active subjects in economic undertaking. In contrast, Sani Yi women were traditionally restricted in domestic life and agricultural responsibilities. The conventional constraints on women have resulted in women’s subordinate status. With growing social change, an increasing number of women presently enter into the market to participate in economic activities. The research shows that in many cases Sani Yi women are compelled to participate in different types of economic activities when their husbands are unable to provide enough economic support for their families. Meanwhile, the fact that economic participation entails change in the women’s lives and in relations with men cannot be ignored. The engagement between Lessinger’s idea of ‘sacrificial motherhood’ and Kabeer’s perspective on ‘women’s empowerment’ draws attention to the key root and the result of women’s economic undertaking. This paper is based on personal narrative approach to explore three Sani women’s experiences and life trajectories. Its objective is to examine the motivation of Sani Yi women’s economic participation. It also draws attention to how each woman’s economic engagement allows her to transform the household gendered division of labor, acquire decision-making power, break the limitations on women’s spatial movement, change women’s attitude towards domestic violence, and gain social status. The study leads to the conclusion that the conventional limitations on Sani Yi women are destabilized gradually even it still continues to exist, and gender relations between Sani Yi women and men are not fixed and rigid but flexible and dynamic.

Keywords: Economic Participation, Gender Relations, Sacrificial Motherhood, Sani Yi Women Embroidery Traders, Women’s Empowerment.

THE POLITICS OF ABDUCTION IN AMRITA PRITAM’S PINJAR AND RUSKIN BOND’S A FLIGHT OF PIGEO

Dr. Parineeta Singh
Assistant Professor, Jaypee Institute of Information Technology, Sector 62, Noida, UP, India

Abstract: The chaos of Partition with its climate of political turmoil and disorder has proved fertile ground for authors setting works, with themes of abduction and rape, within it. Bapsi Sidhwa’s The IceCandy Man and Khushwant Singh’s Train to Pakistan come most forcibly to mind. These books, which depict the cruelty and barbarism of human beings, draw much of their force by focusing on the destructive effects of mass passions. Even Keki N. Daruwalla’s splendid Love across the Salt Desert draws much of its force from the fact that the elopement happens across a political border in times of social unrest. A Flight of Pigeons is another such book set during the mob violence of the Sepoy Mutiny. Such themes have been popular with writers simply because they allow them to put forth the human condition in all its naked truth. This paper seeks to examine the violence perpetrated on women and the treatment of their bodies as sites of contest through a critical study of two novellas dealing with abduction set during times of mob violence - Amrita Pritam’s Pinjar (The Skeleton) and Ruskin Bond’s A Flight of Pigeons. The similarity of form (both are actually somewhat long short stories) and the similarity of content (abduction during mass unrest) belie the difference in the treatment of the subjectmatter by both these literary luminaries. The aim of the paper is thus threefold. First, to critique the novellas in terms of their similarity and assert how they deal with the body as a site of encroachment. The second, is to compare their difference in treatment and to analyze how Bond and Pritam approach the content through drastically different perspectives, and lastly to reflect on the role of gender politics to which this difference in treatment is ascribed.

Keywords: Body, Gender Politics, Partition, Race Relations

EXTRACTION OF CELLULOSE NANO-WHISKERS (CNWS) USING IONIC LIQUID (ILS) ASSISTED ULTRA-SONICATIO

Dr. Zaira Zaman Chowdhury
Nanotechnology and Catalysis Research Center (NANOCAT),
University of Malaya, Malaysia

Abstract: This study focuses on the extraction of cellulose nano whiskers (CNWs) from the leaves of Adansonia kilima (AK), usually known as African Baobab, using a combination of a microwave-assisted alkali (KOH) pre-treatment with subsequent bleaching process prior to ultra-sonication. Ultra-sonication was carried out using ionic liquid (ILs) of1butyl 3 methylimidazolium hydrogen sulfate (Bmim-HSO4). Process parameters for ultra-sonication were optimized using 2-Level Factorial Box Behnken Design (BBD).Process variables such as ultra-sonication power (x1), hydrolysing time (x2) and temperature (x3) were varied. Responses selected were percentage crystallinity index, CrI % (y 1) and yield % (y 1) for the finally procured CNWs sample. Regression analysis was carried out to develop quadratic model to analyse the effect of process variables on ILs assisted ultra-sonication process. Abstract: Indian colleges grant nearly a million engineering degrees annually, flooding the job market with a huge supply of engineers, with skills that have very little demand. With growth of world population exceeding 9 billion, basic needs such as energy, food and water, housing, transportation and health will become more acute.
